Most Generic Pasta

Ingredients

  • 300-400g (about 10-14 oz) of your preferred generic pasta (e.g., penne, fusilli, spaghetti, rotini)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 x 400g (14 oz) can crushed tomatoes or diced tomatoes
  • 1/2 tsp dried oregano (optional)
  • Pinch of red pepper flakes (optional, for a little kick)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves, torn or chopped (optional, for garnish)
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (optional, for serving)

Instructions

  1. Cook the Pasta:
    1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a rolling boil.
    2. Add the pasta and cook according to package directions until al dente (firm to the bite).
    3. Before draining, reserve about 1/2 cup of the pasta cooking water. Drain the pasta and set aside.
  2. Prepare the Sauce:
    1. While the pasta is cooking, heat the olive oil in a large skillet or saucepan over medium heat.
    2. Add the minced garlic and cook for about 30 seconds to 1 minute until fragrant. Be careful not to burn the garlic.
    3. Pour in the crushed or diced tomatoes. Stir in the dried oregano and red pepper flakes (if using).
    4. Bring the sauce to a gentle simmer, then reduce the heat to low and let it cook for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, allowing the flavors to meld.
    5. Season the sauce with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste.
  3. Combine Pasta and Sauce:
    1. Add the cooked and drained pasta directly to the skillet with the tomato sauce.
    2. Toss well to coat the pasta evenly with the sauce. If the sauce seems too thick, add a spoonful or two of the reserved pasta cooking water until it reaches your desired consistency.
    3. Cook for another 1-2 minutes, tossing constantly, to allow the pasta to absorb some of the sauce's flavor.
  4. Serve:
    1. Divide the pasta among serving plates.
    2. Garnish with fresh basil leaves, if desired.
    3. Serve hot, with grated Parmesan cheese on the side for sprinkling, if desired.

Enjoy your simple and delicious pasta!
